Featured Module: Coffee Makes the World Go Round

A short demo showing how a dataset (in this case randomly generated) can be used to control arbitrary animation behavior. Here we randomly assign each bean a position, orbital plane, and orbital speed. Each time-step, we calculate the bean's new position on the plane. I found that due to natural non-linearities in polar coordinates, certain angles wouldn't be covered by a random normalized angle vector. By randomly choosing beans and crossing their orbital plane with the y axis, we can create the illusion of a more uniform global bean distribution.
